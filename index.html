Welcome to Quantosaurus 🦖

A free, open-source, browser-based cell counter.

What is it?

Quantosaurus is a free, browser-based cell counter designed for rapid biological image analysis. It leverages interactive pixel classification—allowing you to train a lightweight machine learning model simply by painting on the canvas. It serves as a rapid, no-code cell counting tool that works beautifully for everything from Brightfield cell counting to fluorescence and DAPI cell counting.

🔒 Local & Private

Your images never leave your computer. All processing happens entirely client-side. No patient data or proprietary lab images are ever uploaded to a server.

When to use Quantosaurus?

Don't reach immediately for Quantosaurus; use the right tool for the job:

  • Use ImageJ/Fiji when: You just need simple, global thresholding.
  • Use Cellpose when: You have complex, overlapping cells and the time/hardware to setup a neural net.
  • Use Quantosaurus when: Thresholding fails, but you don't want to code. You just want to paint a few examples, train a lightweight model, and get a count instantly.

Instructions

1
Choose Images
Drag your microscopy images (.tif, .png, .jpg) directly onto the canvas or use the file picker.
2
Paint Examples
Select the 🖌 Paint tool. Mark a few cells as 'Class 1' (Foreground) and mark the empty space as 'Class 2' (Background). The model only needs a few samples to start learning!
3
Get Counts
Adjust the Feature Scale (Sigma) if your cells are fuzzy or large. The model trains automatically. Counts appear next to their classes!

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: How do I get better segmentation results?

A: Ensure you provide diverse examples. Paint both the bright centers and dim edges of cells, and explicitly label artifacts or dust as background (Class 2).

Q: My browser crashed on export, why?

A: Very large images (e.g., uncompressed .TIFs > 50MB) can exceed browser memory limits. Try downscaling your images before importing if you experience crashes.
