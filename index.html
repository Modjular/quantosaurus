Count cells in your browser — free, private, no install

Quantosaurus is an open-source tool for counting cells and analyzing microscopy images. Everything runs on your own machine using your GPU — from a simple brightness threshold to the Cellpose deep-learning model. Pick the tool that fits your image and get a count in seconds.

🔒 Your images never leave your computer — all processing is client-side.

Choose your tool

🎚️ Easy

Threshold

A single brightness cutoff, chosen automatically (Otsu) or by hand. Instant, no training. Best when your objects are clearly brighter or darker than the background and don't touch.

Pixel Classifier

Paint a few example pixels and a lightweight model learns to classify the rest, live. Best when a plain threshold fails — uneven lighting, textured backgrounds, or subtle contrast.

Cellpose

The pretrained cyto3 deep-learning model, running in your browser on WebGPU. No training needed. Best for crowded, touching, or overlapping cells that other methods merge together.

Frequently asked questions

Do my images get uploaded anywhere?

No. Quantosaurus has no backend — every image loads, processes, and exports entirely inside your browser tab. Nothing is sent to a server, so it's safe for unpublished data, patient images, or proprietary lab work.

What image formats are supported?

TIFF (.tif/.tiff, including 16-bit and multichannel), plus PNG and JPG. Only 2D images are supported today.

Do I need a special browser?

Threshold and the Pixel Classifier work in any modern browser (WebGPU with an automatic WebGL2 fallback). Cellpose needs WebGPU — use a recent Chrome or Edge for it.

Is it really free?

Yes — free and open source (MIT). No signup, no account, no limits.
